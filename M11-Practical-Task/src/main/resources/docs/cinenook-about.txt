About CineNook

CineNook is a members-only film club and small independent cinema based in the Old Town of Vilnius, Lithuania. It was founded in 2018 by two film students, Juste Norkute and Tomas Aleknavicius, who wanted a cosy place to watch and discuss films that rarely reach big cinemas.

The club's mission is to celebrate classic, independent and international cinema, and to build a community of curious film lovers. CineNook is named after its "nook" — a snug 60-seat screening room tucked behind a bookshop on Pilies Street.

Unlike a commercial multiplex, CineNook does not show blockbusters on opening weekend. Instead it curates themed programmes, hosts director Q&A evenings, and runs an annual short film competition. All screenings are chosen by a small volunteer programming committee.

CineNook is a non-profit organisation funded by membership fees, cafe sales and occasional grants from the Lithuanian Film Centre. It is run mostly by volunteers, with two part-time staff members.
